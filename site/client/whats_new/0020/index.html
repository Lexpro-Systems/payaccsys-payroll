New Once-Off Payment Tax Calculation Method

A new, more accurate, method for calculating PAYE on once-off payslip items (such as bonus or annual payments) is available.

In the past, the annualised method of calculating PAYE on once-off payments was used exclusively. This method entails that the basic salary plus the bonus amount is annualised to determine the annual taxable amount.

For example, if an employee earns a salary of 15,000.00 per month and receives a bonus of 15,000.00, the PAYE will be calculated as follows:

15,000 (salary) + 15,000 (bonus) = 30,000 (total income for the month)
30,000 * 12 (payment periods in the tax year) = 360,000 (total income for the year)
According to the tax-table for 2024/2025, the PAYE on 360,000 = 57,397.00

PAYE for the payslip: 57,397.00 / 12 = 4,783.08

The new method, called the balance of remuneration method, calculates tax more accurately by determining the difference between the tax without the bonus and the tax including the bonus. For example, given the same scenario as above, tax will be calculated as follows:

15,000 (salary) * 12 (payment periods in the tax year) = 180,000
PAYE on 180,000 = 15,165

180,000 (salary for the tax year) + 15,000 (bonus) = 195,000
PAYE on 195,000 = 17,865

PAYE on bonus: 17,865 - 15,165 = 2,700
PAYE on salary: 15,165 / 12 = 1,263.75

PAYE for the payslip: 1,263.75 (PAYE on salary) + 2,700 (PAYE on bonus) = 3,963.75

Note that the annualised method will still be used for all existing companies. Please contact your consultant if you wish to use the balance of remuneration method instead.
